Estimating Demand and Market Outcomes

Title: Demand Estimation for Oatmeal

This study examines consumer demand for oatmeal products in order to understand how pricing decisions influence both revenue and customer welfare in a differentiated grocery category. Using more than 750,000 observations from Dominick’s supermarket scanner data, the analysis applies a Berry logit framework with extensive fixed effects to estimate price elasticities, identify substitution patterns, and measure variation in consumer responsiveness across product types. The motivation is to provide retailers and category managers with an evidence‑based view of how consumers react to price changes and product attributes, particularly in settings where simple correlations fail to capture endogenous pricing or unobserved quality differences. The results show an average own‑price elasticity of about 1.75 and significantly higher sensitivity for instant oatmeal products. Counterfactual simulations quantify the impact of uniform price increases, targeted adjustments, and package size changes on revenue and welfare, revealing consistent numerical tradeoffs between profitability and consumer benefit. The findings demonstrate how elasticity variation and substitution dynamics determine the outcomes of practical pricing strategies.

Sports Betting & Bankruptcy Research

Title: An Analysis on Bankruptcy and Mortgage Delinquency after Online Sports Betting Legalization

An empirical paper studying the effect of online sports betting legalization on bankruptcy rates in the U.S. Visit the abstract of the paper for a summary of my findings, methodology, and analysis.

Briefly, this paper is motivated by the rapid rise of online sports betting in the United States following the U.S. Supreme Court overturning the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act (PASPA) in 2018. Since then, modern media has extensively covered both the benefits and downsides of online sports betting. A troubling contradiction exists within the sports world itself as while professional betting scandals become increasingly prevalent, these same leagues, on the other hand, continue to sell their advertisement rights to the very sportsbooks involved. Regardless, I hope that my findings meaningfully contribute to the ongoing policy debate surrounding the legalization of sports betting.
