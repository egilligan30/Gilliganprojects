01 — About Me

I'm a 4th year Economics undergraduate at UVA with a strong interest in applied research, predictive methods, and understanding how markets and policies shape real-world outcomes.

My academic focus spans industrial organization, behavioral and experimental economics, and econometrics. As such, I enjoy working at the intersection of economic theory and data.

I currently serve as a Research Assistant in the UVA Department of Economics, collaborating with faculty on projects in behavioral economics and applied/historical IO. My work includes data construction and cleaning, regressions and data analysis, experiment design, and feedback on empirical strategies.

In my coursework and independent projects, I've built large-scale structural demand models using supermarket scanner data, conducted causal analyses with panel datasets to evaluate policy impacts, leveraged Chebyshev Distance and Monte Carlo simulations to predict March Madness, and developed behavioral simulations in Python using oTree.

To complete these projects, I have leveraged my experience in Python and Stata for data construction and analysis, and my knowledge of R and SQL to harmoninze, clean, and visualize (ggplot) datasets.

I'm interested in pursuing graduate study in economics or a related field and generally using quantitative methods to predict and optimize market outcomes.
