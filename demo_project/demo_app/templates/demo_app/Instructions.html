Game Instructions

Welcome to the simulation! In this experiment, you will manage your finances across {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} rounds by placing bets with different odds.

1. Starting Conditions

  • You begin with $250 in savings and $0 in debt.
  • Each round, you must pay a mortgage of $25.
  • If you cannot cover the mortgage from your savings, the unpaid amount will be added to your debt.
  • If your total debt reaches $500, you go bankrupt and the game ends.

2. Placing Bets

  • Each round, you will choose:
    • A bet amount between $0 and $250.
    • Bet odds using a slider ranging from negative to positive values.
  • Negative odds (e.g. -200) represent safer bets that are more likely to win, but pay less.
  • Positive odds (e.g. +200) represent riskier bets that are less likely to win, but pay more.

3. Example Payouts (for a $10 bet)

  • Odds -200: Win $15, Lose $10
  • Odds -100: Win $20, Lose $10
  • Odds 0 (even): Win $10, Lose $10
  • Odds +100: Win $20, Lose $10
  • Odds +200: Win $30, Lose $10

4. After Each Round

  • Your savings and debt will be updated based on your bet result and the mortgage payment.
  • Your current financial status will carry over to the next round.
  • The range of possible odds may increase in later rounds.

5. Winning and Losing

  • If you keep your debt below $500 after all {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} rounds, you have survived financially!
  • If your debt reaches $500 or more, you are bankrupt and the game ends immediately.

6. Other

  • Points and dollars are synonomous; please do not think that one has more value than the other.
